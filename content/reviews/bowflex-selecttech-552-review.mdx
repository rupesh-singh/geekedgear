## The Quick Verdict

The Bowflex SelectTech 552s are **the most versatile adjustable dumbbells** you can buy for a home gym. 15 weight increments (5–52.5 lbs), smooth dial adjustment, and a compact footprint that replaces an entire dumbbell rack. They're not perfect — the plastic housing makes some people nervous, and you can't slam them — but for 90% of home gym lifters, these are the move.

**Rating: 4.7/5** — Best all-around adjustable dumbbell for most people.

## Who This Is For

- Home gym owners who want variety without buying 15+ pairs of dumbbells
- Beginners to intermediate lifters (the 52.5 lb max is the only limitation for advanced lifters)
- People with limited space — these take up about 2 square feet

## Who Should Skip This

- Advanced lifters who regularly press 60+ lbs per hand (look at the 1090s instead)
- People who train exclusively with barbells
- Anyone who drops their dumbbells regularly (these will break)

## What I Liked

### The dial system is genuinely clever
Turn the dial, lift the dumbbell, done. It takes about 3 seconds to change weight. In a circuit workout, this is a game-changer compared to fumbling with plates or pins.

### 15 weight increments
5, 7.5, 10, 12.5, 15, 17.5, 20, 22.5, 25, 30, 35, 40, 45, 50, 52.5 lbs. The 2.5 lb increments at the lower end are perfect for progressive overload on isolation exercises.

### Compact footprint
The cradle is about 16" × 8". That's it. You'd need an entire wall of dumbbells to match the range these provide.

## What I Didn't Like

### The plastic housing
The outer shell is plastic, which feels less premium than the all-metal PowerBlock alternatives. I've never had durability issues in 6 months, but it doesn't inspire confidence when you're lifting heavy.

### Can't drop them
These are NOT meant to be dropped. If you fail a heavy set and let go, you'll break the adjustment mechanism. For bench press, I always have a plan B.

### The length at lower weights
At 5–10 lbs, the dumbbell is still full-sized (the unused weight plates stay in the cradle, but the bar length stays the same). It's a bit awkward for some exercises.

The **PowerBlock Elite** is the main alternative worth considering. It's more durable (all steel, 10-year warranty) and has a smaller footprint, but the pin adjustment is slightly slower and the shape takes getting used to.

## Bottom Line

For most home gym owners, the Bowflex 552 is still the best adjustable dumbbell you can buy. The dial adjustment is the fastest, the weight range covers 90% of exercises, and the footprint is tiny. Just don't drop them.

**Buy if:** You want the most convenient, versatile adjustable dumbbell for general training.

**Skip if:** You're an advanced lifter who needs 60+ lbs, or you have a habit of dropping weights.
