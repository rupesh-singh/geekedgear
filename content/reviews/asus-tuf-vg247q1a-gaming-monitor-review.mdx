## The Quick Verdict

The ASUS TUF Gaming VG247Q1A is **the best budget 165Hz gaming monitor you can buy in India** right now. It combines a 23.8" Full HD panel with ASUS's ELMB technology for a true 1ms response time, FreeSync Premium for tear-free gaming, and Shadow Boost for better dark-scene visibility. At ₹13,499 (down from ₹22,999), it's a steal.

**Rating: 4.6/5** — Best value gaming monitor under ₹15K.

## Who This Is For

- Competitive gamers who want 165Hz smoothness on a budget
- Console gamers with PS5/Xbox Series X who want a dedicated gaming display
- Anyone upgrading from a 60Hz monitor and wanting a massive visual upgrade

## Who Should Skip This

- Content creators who need color-accurate wide-gamut displays (look at LG 24GN65R for sRGB 99%)
- People who want 4K or 1440p resolution — this is 1080p only
- Multi-monitor users who need ultra-thin bezels

## What I Liked

### 165Hz is buttery smooth
Coming from 60Hz, the jump to 165Hz is immediately noticeable in every game. Menu scrolling, mouse movement, and fast-paced shooters all feel dramatically smoother. It also supports 144Hz for compatibility with older setups.

### ELMB technology eliminates ghosting
ASUS's Extreme Low Motion Blur technology delivers a 1ms (MPRT) response time that actually works. In fast-paced FPS games, ghosting is essentially invisible. What sets this apart from competitors is that ELMB works alongside Adaptive-Sync — most monitors force you to choose one or the other.

### Shadow Boost is genuinely useful
This isn't marketing fluff. Shadow Boost brightens dark areas without over-exposing bright regions. In games like Valorant and Apex, spotting enemies in dark corners becomes noticeably easier.

### Built-in speakers (yes, really)
They're not audiophile-grade, but having built-in speakers means you can game without headphones for casual sessions. Most monitors at this price skip speakers entirely.

### FreeSync Premium with NVIDIA compatibility
Works with both AMD FreeSync and NVIDIA Adaptive-Sync (GTX 10 series and newer). No tearing, no stuttering, regardless of your GPU brand.

## What I Didn't Like

### VA panel, not IPS
This is a VA panel, which means slightly narrower viewing angles compared to IPS. Colors shift a bit when viewed from extreme angles. For a straight-on gaming setup, this is barely noticeable, but side-by-side with an IPS panel, you'll see the difference.

### Stand is basic
You get tilt adjustment only — no height, swivel, or pivot. For a monitor at this price, that's expected, but if ergonomics matter, budget ₹1,500 for a VESA monitor arm.

### 1080p at 24 inches
At 24 inches, 1080p is perfectly sharp for gaming. But if you're doing productivity work with lots of text, a 1440p panel would be nicer. For the price range though, 1080p + high refresh rate is the right trade-off.

The ASUS TUF Gaming VG247Q1A nails the fundamentals: high refresh rate, low response time, FreeSync Premium, and built-in speakers — all under ₹14K. The VA panel is the only compromise, and for most gamers, it's one they won't notice.
